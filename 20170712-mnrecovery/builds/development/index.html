Narrowing gaps in personal income
Since the end of the Great Recession spanning 2007 to 2009, average per capita personal income in metro area and outstate counties drew closer together.
Source: U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis
Personal income growth by county
Personal income grew by larger percentages from 2009 to 2015 in many non-metro counties than in the metro, especially in the southwest.

Changes in household income by county
About 50 Minnesota counties saw median household income grow at equal or greater percentages than the state average of 3 percent, mostly outside the metro area. Some of those below average still saw some growth.
Source: U.S. Census Bureau, Minnesota Compass
Median income growth by region
Measuring the percent change of median household income between 2009 and 2015 shows the Twin Cities metro below much of Greater Minnesota and beneath the state average.
Source: Minnesota Compass
Rising outstate tax revenue
While a majority of Minnesota's tax revenue is still generated by metro counties, outstate counties tax revenue has increased as incomes have recovered.
Source: Minnesota House of Representatives Research Department
Economic recovery by county
Many outstate counties saw more economic indicators grow above the state average between 2009 and 2015 than in the metro area. Indicators tracked are changes to percentages of children in poverty, jobs, personal income, household income and housing price indexes.
Source: Star Tribune analysis
Economic recovery in Trump country
About half the counties that voted for Barack Obama in 2012 and flipped to Donald Trump in the 2016 presidential contest saw growth above the state average on three or more economic indicators.
Source: National Association of Counties
