Narrowing gaps in personal income
Since the Great Recession spanning 2007 to 2009, average personal income in metro area and outstate counties have gradually started to converge.
Source: U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis
Personal income growth by county
Personal income grew by larger percentages from 2009 to 2015 in many non-metro counties than in the metro, especially in the southwest.
Source: U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis

Changes in household income by county
About 50 Minnesota counties saw median household income grow at greater percentages than the state average of 3 percent, mostly outside the metro area. Some of those below average still saw some growth.
Source: U.S. Census Bureau, Minnesota Compass
Rising outstate tax revenue
While a majority of Minnesota's tax revenue is still generated by metro counties, outstate counties tax revenue has increased as incomes have recovered.
Source: Minnesota House of Representatives Research Department
Changes in taxes paid per capita 2009-2013
Changes in taxes and government aid per capita in Minnesota counties show some of the greatest shifts in non-metro counties.

Economic recovery by county
In many cases, outstate counties recovered at rates above the state average on more economic indicators than those in the metro area. Indicators tracked are changes to percentages of children in poverty, jobs, personal income, household income and wage recovery.
Source: Star Tribune analysis
Economic recovery in Trump country
About half the counties that flipped from Democrat to Republican in 2016's presidential contest had previously recovered their jobs. Even those that did not still saw increases in personal income.
Source: National Association of Counties
