Climbing Minnesota suicides
Suicides in Minnesota had remained reasonably flat and reached their lowest point in 2000, after which they started to increase steadily.

Narrowing with the national average
Since 1981, Minnesota's suicide rate had been lower than the U.S. average until the mid-2000s when it started converging with national trends.

Minnesota suicides by age
From 1981 to 2015, those who have died by suicide in Minnesota have been overwhelmingly male and aged 20 to 49-years-old.
Source: Minnesota Department of Health, WISQARS, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention | Graphic by Jeff Hargarten, Star Tribune | Download Data
Minnesota suicide trends by age
Since 2000, the age group with the fastest growing suicide rate was 50 to 64-year-olds. Also shows trends for those aged 0 to 19, 20 to 34, 35 to 49 and 65 or older.
Source: Minnesota Department of Health, WISQARS, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention | Graphic by Jeff Hargarten, Star Tribune | Download Data
Minnesota suicides by gender
Suicide deaths among women had dropped significantly by 2000 before steadily rising again. The vast majority of those dying by suicide in Minnesota and nationally are still men.
Source: Minnesota Department of Health, WISQARS, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention | Graphic by Jeff Hargarten, Star Tribune | Download Data
Minnesota suicides by method
Firearms, suffocation and poisoning were the leading methods of suicide in Minnesota from 1999 to 2014.
